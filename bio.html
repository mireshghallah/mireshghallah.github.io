Niloofar Mireshghallah is a Research Scientist in the Alignment group at Meta’s Fundamental AI Research (FAIR) lab and will join Carnegie Mellon University in Fall 2026 as an Assistant Professor jointly appointed in the Language Technologies Institute (LTI) and the Department of Engineering & Public Policy (EPP). Previously, she was a post-doctoral scholar at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington. She received her Ph.D. in Computer Science from UC San Diego in 2023. Her research focuses on privacy in machine learning, natural-language processing, and the legal and societal implications of generative AI. Niloofar’s work has been recognized with the NCWIT Collegiate Award (2020), finalist distinction in the Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship (2021), the Rising Star in Adversarial ML Award (2022), and selection for the Rising Stars in EECS workshop (2022).
